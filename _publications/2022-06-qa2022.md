In our recent work on the Qur&apos;an QA 2023 shared tasks A and B, we tackled the challenge of limited training data by leveraging transfer learning and a voting ensemble to enhance prediction stability across multiple runs. We employed diverse architectures and learning mechanisms using various Arabic pre-trained transformer-based models for both tasks. To effectively identify unanswerable questions, we implemented a thresholding mechanism. Our top-performing systems significantly outperformed the baseline, achieving a MAP score of 25.05% for task A and a partial Average Precision (pAP) of 57.11% for task B, showcasing the robustness and effectiveness of our approach.
